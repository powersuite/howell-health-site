Florida Blue vs. United Healthcare vs. Cigna: How to Choose the Right Carrier

By Scott Howell • April 15, 2026 • Licensed Independent Health Insurance Agent

Florida Blue, United Healthcare, and Cigna are three of the top health insurance carriers in Florida, but each excels in different areas. Florida Blue offers the largest in-state provider network, United Healthcare provides the strongest nationwide coverage, and Cigna stands out for international options and wellness programs. The right choice depends on your doctors, travel patterns, budget, and coverage priorities — which is why comparing all three side by side is essential.

Why the Carrier You Choose Matters

Many people assume all health insurance is the same — you pay a premium and get coverage. In reality, your choice of carrier affects nearly every aspect of your healthcare experience: which doctors you can see, which hospitals are covered, how much you pay for prescriptions, what your out-of-pocket costs look like, and how easy it is to get claims resolved.

Choosing the wrong carrier can mean paying out-of-network rates to see your current doctor, getting stuck with a narrow formulary that doesn't cover your medications, or dealing with poor customer service when you need help most. Here's how the three major Florida carriers compare.

Florida Blue: The In-State Powerhouse

Florida Blue is the state's Blue Cross Blue Shield affiliate and the dominant health insurance carrier in Florida. It's the carrier most Floridians know by name, and for good reason — it has the largest in-state presence of any insurer.

Florida Blue Strengths

  • Largest Florida network: Over 60,000 providers and contracts with nearly every hospital in the state. If you want the broadest choice of local doctors and facilities, Florida Blue typically leads.
  • Marketplace dominance: Florida Blue is available on the ACA marketplace in most Florida counties, often offering more plan options than competitors.
  • Blue Cross Blue Shield national access: Through the BlueCard program, Florida Blue members can access the BCBS network nationwide, which includes over 90% of hospitals and doctors in the U.S.
  • Strong local presence: Physical retail centers across Florida where you can get in-person help with enrollment and questions.
  • Established reputation: Decades of operating in Florida means deep relationships with local health systems.

Florida Blue Considerations

  • Marketplace premiums can be higher in some counties compared to competitors
  • Plan designs can be complex, with multiple network tiers (Blue Options, Blue Select, etc.)
  • Some plans have narrower networks despite the carrier's overall large network — always verify the specific plan

United Healthcare: The National Network Giant

United Healthcare (UHC) is the largest health insurance company in the United States by membership. In Florida, it offers both marketplace and private PPO plans with competitive pricing and strong technology tools.

United Healthcare Strengths

  • Largest national network: Over 1.3 million healthcare professionals and 6,700+ hospitals nationwide. If you travel frequently or split time between states, UHC's network depth is hard to beat.
  • Strong technology platform: The UHC mobile app and member portal are among the best in the industry for finding providers, checking claims, and managing benefits.
  • Competitive private PPO pricing: United Healthcare often offers some of the most competitive rates on private, medically underwritten PPO plans in Florida.
  • Pharmacy network: OptumRx (UHC's pharmacy benefit manager) provides extensive pharmacy access and competitive drug pricing.
  • Telehealth integration: Virtual care options are well-integrated into plans, often at reduced or $0 copay.

United Healthcare Considerations

  • Not available on the ACA marketplace in all Florida counties
  • Some Florida providers still prefer Florida Blue's contracts — verify your specific doctors
  • Plan options can vary significantly by region within Florida

Cigna: The Wellness and International Leader

Cigna has a growing presence in Florida and differentiates itself through comprehensive wellness programs, strong behavioral health coverage, and excellent international options.

Cigna Strengths

  • Wellness and preventive focus: Cigna's wellness programs, including health coaching, rewards for healthy behaviors, and comprehensive preventive care, are among the most robust in the industry.
  • Behavioral health coverage: Cigna's Evernorth behavioral health network is one of the largest in the country, with strong access to mental health and substance abuse treatment providers.
  • International coverage: For contractors, remote workers, or frequent international travelers, Cigna Global offers health plans that work seamlessly worldwide.
  • Competitive group rates: Cigna is often very competitive for small group and employer-sponsored plans in Florida.
  • Express Scripts pharmacy: Through its Evernorth pharmacy services, Cigna offers competitive prescription pricing and home delivery options.

Cigna Considerations

  • Smaller Florida provider network compared to Florida Blue — verify your doctors carefully
  • Limited marketplace availability in some Florida counties
  • Individual plan options may be fewer than competitors in certain areas

Side-by-Side Comparison

Here's how the three carriers stack up across the most important factors:

  • In-state network size: Florida Blue (largest) > United Healthcare > Cigna
  • National network size: United Healthcare (largest) > Florida Blue (via BlueCard) > Cigna
  • International coverage: Cigna (strongest) > United Healthcare > Florida Blue
  • Marketplace availability: Florida Blue (most counties) > Cigna > United Healthcare
  • Private PPO pricing: Varies by individual factors — must compare quotes
  • Technology/app experience: United Healthcare > Cigna > Florida Blue
  • Wellness programs: Cigna (strongest) > United Healthcare > Florida Blue
  • Mental health access: Cigna (strongest) > United Healthcare > Florida Blue

How to Choose the Right Carrier for You

The "best" carrier is entirely personal. Here's a decision framework:

Choose Florida Blue if:

  • Your preferred doctors and hospitals are in the Florida Blue network
  • You want the widest choice of in-state providers
  • You're shopping on the ACA marketplace and want the most plan options
  • You value an established carrier with physical locations for in-person support

Choose United Healthcare if:

  • You travel frequently or live in multiple states during the year
  • You want a strong nationwide provider network
  • You're looking for competitive private PPO plan pricing
  • You value a top-tier mobile app and digital tools for managing your plan

Choose Cigna if:

  • You travel internationally or work abroad
  • You prioritize wellness programs and preventive care incentives
  • You need strong behavioral health or mental health coverage
  • You're a small business owner evaluating group plan options

Don't Forget About Other Carriers

While Florida Blue, UHC, and Cigna are the Big Three, other carriers also serve Florida and may be the best fit for specific situations:

  • Aetna (CVS Health): Strong pharmacy integration through CVS MinuteClinic and competitive pricing in select Florida markets
  • Ambetter (Centene): Focused on the ACA marketplace with some of the lowest-premium options available in Florida
  • Oscar Health: Technology-forward carrier with a simple user experience, available on the marketplace in select Florida counties

Frequently Asked Questions

Which carrier has the largest provider network in Florida?

Florida Blue has the largest provider network within the state, with over 60,000 healthcare providers and contracts with nearly every hospital in Florida. United Healthcare has the largest national network, with over 1.3 million providers nationwide. Cigna offers strong international coverage and a growing Florida network. The best network for you depends on which doctors and hospitals you need access to.

Is Florida Blue cheaper than United Healthcare or Cigna?

Pricing varies significantly by age, location, plan type, and coverage level. Florida Blue often offers competitive marketplace pricing due to its dominant market position. United Healthcare and Cigna may offer better rates on private PPO plans for healthy individuals. The only way to know which is cheapest for your situation is to compare quotes side by side.

Can I have doctors from different carrier networks?

No, you can only use in-network providers from the carrier and plan you're enrolled in. However, PPO plans do provide some out-of-network coverage at reduced benefit levels. If you need to see providers across multiple carrier networks, you'll need to choose the carrier whose network includes your most important providers.

Which carrier is best for people who travel frequently?

United Healthcare typically offers the strongest nationwide coverage with its extensive national network. Cigna is the best choice for international travelers, with robust global coverage options. Florida Blue's network is strongest within Florida but provides national access through the BlueCard program. Private PPO plans from any major carrier generally offer broader travel coverage than HMO plans.
