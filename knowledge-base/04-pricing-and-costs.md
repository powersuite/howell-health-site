## Private PPO vs. ACA Cost Comparison

### Premium Savings with Private PPO
- For healthy individuals not qualifying for subsidies, private PPO premiums can be **30-50% lower** than comparable ACA marketplace plans
- A family of four paying $1,200+/month on the marketplace may find comparable or better private PPO coverage for **$700-$1,000/month**
- Palm Bay residents: private PPO saves **$150-$300/month** compared to marketplace options

### When ACA Is Cheaper
- When you qualify for premium subsidies (income 100-400% of federal poverty level)
- Subsidies can reduce a $650/month Silver plan to **$100 or less**
- Some self-employed with lower incomes qualify for **$0-$50/month** plans
- Family of 4 earning $60,000 may qualify for **$800-$1,200/month** in subsidies

### Total Cost Matters (Not Just Premium)
Always calculate the full annual picture:
- Monthly premium x 12
- Annual deductible
- Expected copays
- Coinsurance percentage
- Out-of-pocket maximum

A plan with a higher premium but lower deductible might save thousands overall if you use healthcare regularly.

## ACA Subsidy Information

### Who Qualifies
- Household income between **100% and 400%** of the federal poverty level
- **Single individual (2026):** Roughly $15,000-$62,000/year
- **Family of four (2026):** Up to approximately $127,000/year

### How Subsidies Work
- Advance Premium Tax Credits (APTCs) reduce monthly premiums
- Based on the second-lowest-cost Silver plan (benchmark) in your area
- Applied at enrollment to lower your monthly bill

### Cost-Sharing Reductions (CSR)
- Available if income is below **250% of the federal poverty level** AND you choose a Silver plan
- Lowers deductibles, copays, and out-of-pocket maximums without increasing premiums

### 2026 Subsidy Cliff Warning
The Enhanced Premium Tax Credits from the Inflation Reduction Act are set to expire in 2026. Without congressional action:
- Many families earning above 400% FPL will **lose subsidies entirely**
- Those below the threshold will see **reduced assistance**
- Expected to drive significant marketplace premium increases
- Private PPO plans become especially attractive for those losing subsidies

### Self-Employed Subsidy Strategy
- Self-employed individuals' subsidies are based on MAGI (Modified Adjusted Gross Income)
- More business expenses deducted = lower MAGI = larger subsidy
- SEP IRA, Solo 401(k), and traditional IRA contributions reduce MAGI
- Increases subsidy eligibility while building retirement savings

## Cost of Being Uninsured

### Common Procedures at Full Retail (No Insurance)

**Emergency and Urgent Care:**
- ER visit: $2,500-$5,000
- ER with imaging: $4,000-$8,000
- Urgent care visit: $250-$600
- Ambulance transport: $1,200-$2,500

**Hospital Stays and Surgeries:**
- 3-day hospital stay: $30,000-$75,000
- Appendectomy: $15,000-$35,000
- ACL repair: $20,000-$50,000
- Heart attack treatment: $50,000-$150,000+
- Childbirth (vaginal): $10,000-$20,000
- C-section delivery: $20,000-$42,000

**Routine and Preventive (Without Insurance):**
- Primary care visit: $150-$350
- Specialist visit: $250-$500
- Blood work panel: $200-$800
- MRI scan: $1,500-$3,500
- Mammogram: $250-$500
- Colonoscopy: $2,000-$4,500

**With Insurance:**
- Annual out-of-pocket maximum typically $4,000-$9,000/year no matter what happens

### Real-World Family Scenarios

**Youth Sports Injury (ACL Tear):**
- Uninsured cost: $31,500-$49,500 (MRI + surgery + physical therapy)
- With insurance: Max OOP $4,000-$9,000

**Unexpected Diagnosis (Cancer Scare):**
- Uninsured diagnostics: $3,000-$8,000; treatment if needed: $100,000+
- With insurance: Capped at OOP maximum $8,000-$17,000

**Child's Broken Arm:**
- Uninsured: $8,000-$15,000 (complex: $20,000+)
- With insurance: Copay + OOP typically $500-$2,000

### Financial Ripple Effects of Being Uninsured
- Credit score damage: 100+ point drop from medical collections
- Wage garnishment: Florida allows legal action for medical debt
- Retirement savings depletion
- Home equity risk (liens, forced sale)
- Family stress, divorce, anxiety

### Key Statistic
- **66.5%** of bankruptcies in America are tied to medical issues — approximately **530,000 families per year**
- Florida ranks among the top states for medical debt
- Brevard County: **12-14% of non-elderly population uninsured** (~55,000-65,000 residents)

## COBRA Costs
- Full premium plus 2% administrative fee
- Typically **$600-$1,800/month**
- Usually **40-60% more expensive** than private PPO or marketplace alternatives
- Makes sense: mid-treatment, met your deductible, very short-term bridge
- Almost always better to switch to private PPO or marketplace plan

## Tax Savings for Self-Employed

### Self-Employed Health Insurance Deduction
- Deduct **100%** of health insurance premiums (medical, dental, vision, long-term care)
- **Above-the-line deduction** — available whether you itemize or not
- Reduces adjusted gross income directly

### Tax Savings Examples
- $8,400/year premiums ($700/month) in 22% bracket = **~$1,848 federal tax savings**
- $4,800/year premiums ($400/month) in 22% bracket = **~$1,056 federal tax savings**
- Effective monthly cost of $400/month plan: **~$312** after tax deduction

### What's Deductible
- Medical insurance premiums (self, spouse, dependents)
- Dental insurance premiums
- Vision insurance premiums
- Long-term care insurance premiums (age limits apply)
- Supplemental coverage in some cases

### Important Rules
- Cannot deduct for months you were eligible for employer coverage
- Cannot deduct more than net self-employment income
- Consult a tax professional for your specific situation

## What Affects Your Premium

### Primary Factors
1. **Age** — Single biggest factor (3:1 age band under ACA)
2. **Plan type** — Bronze vs. Silver vs. Gold; PPO vs. HMO
3. **Deductible level** — Lower deductible = higher premium
4. **Location** — Geographic rating area (Brevard County = Rating Area 10)
5. **Number of dependents** — More people covered = higher total premium
6. **Health status** — For private PPO plans (medically underwritten)
7. **Tobacco use** — Up to 50% surcharge on marketplace plans (not covered by subsidies)

### NOT a Factor (for ACA plans)
- Pre-existing conditions
- Gender
- Health history
- Claims history
