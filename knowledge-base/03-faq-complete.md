## General Health Insurance

### What's the difference between private health insurance and ACA marketplace plans?
Private health insurance plans are sold outside the ACA marketplace directly through carriers. They typically offer broader PPO networks with nationwide coverage, no referral requirements, and year-round enrollment. ACA marketplace plans are standardized into metal tiers (Bronze, Silver, Gold, Platinum) and may include premium subsidies based on income. Private plans are medically underwritten (approval based on health history), while ACA plans are guaranteed issue regardless of pre-existing conditions. The best choice depends on your income, health status, and coverage needs.

### How much does health insurance cost in 2026?
For a healthy 40-year-old in Florida, private PPO plans start around $276 per month. Unsubsidized ACA Silver marketplace plans average $652 per month. Family coverage ranges from $700 to $1,500 or more per month depending on ages, plan type, and number of dependents. Your actual cost depends on age, health status, income (for ACA subsidies), and the specific plan you choose.

### How much does health insurance cost in Brevard County, Florida in 2026?
Individual health insurance in Brevard County typically costs between $350 and $900 per month depending on age, plan type, and subsidy eligibility. Family plans range from $900 to $2,500 per month. Brevard County sits in Florida's Rating Area 10, so rates are the same across Melbourne, Palm Bay, Cocoa Beach, Titusville, and Merritt Island.

### What happens if I don't have health insurance and something catastrophic happens?
Without insurance, you pay full retail price for everything. A single ER visit averages $2,500-$5,000, a broken bone can cost $7,500-$15,000, and a hospital stay can exceed $30,000. Medical debt is the number one cause of personal bankruptcy in America. With insurance, your out-of-pocket maximum caps your annual spending — typically $4,000-$9,000 per year no matter what happens.

### What is the real cost of being uninsured in Florida?
Beyond medical bills, being uninsured puts you at risk for credit score damage (100+ points from collections), wage garnishment, retirement savings depletion, and home equity liens. Florida has no state penalty for being uninsured, but the real "penalty" is full financial exposure every single day. Affordable options exist — many people qualify for ACA subsidies that bring premiums to $0-$50/month, and private PPO plans start around $276/month.

### Can I get health insurance if I have a pre-existing condition?
Yes. ACA marketplace plans are guaranteed issue — they cannot deny you coverage for pre-existing conditions. Private PPO plans are medically underwritten, meaning approval is based on health history. If you have a pre-existing condition, I compare both options to determine which provides the best combination of coverage and affordability.

### How quickly can I get health insurance coverage?
Private PPO plans can start as soon as the first of the next month. ACA marketplace plans have specific enrollment periods — open enrollment (November 1 through January 15) or special enrollment after a qualifying life event. I help you find the fastest path to coverage based on your situation.

### What is a health insurance deductible and how does it work?
A deductible is the amount you pay out of pocket before your insurance starts covering costs. For example, with a $3,000 deductible, you pay the first $3,000 of medical expenses yourself. After that, insurance kicks in (usually with copays or coinsurance). Preventive care is typically covered at $0 even before meeting your deductible.

### What is the difference between a copay, coinsurance, and deductible?
- **Deductible:** The amount you pay before insurance starts covering costs
- **Copay:** A fixed dollar amount you pay per visit or service (e.g., $30 for a doctor visit)
- **Coinsurance:** A percentage of costs you share with insurance after the deductible (e.g., you pay 20%, insurance pays 80%)

### What is an out-of-pocket maximum and why does it matter?
The out-of-pocket maximum is the most you'll pay for covered services in a year. Once you hit this cap, insurance covers 100% of remaining costs. It's your safety net — no matter how expensive your medical care gets, your annual costs are capped. Typical OOP maximums range from $4,000 to $9,000.

### Does health insurance cover mental health and therapy?
Yes. All ACA-compliant plans — both marketplace and most private PPO plans — must cover mental health and substance use disorder services as one of the 10 essential health benefits. This includes therapy, counseling, psychiatric services, and inpatient mental health treatment.

### How does health insurance cover prescriptions and what is a formulary?
Each insurance carrier has a formulary — a list of covered medications organized into tiers. Tier 1 (generics) have the lowest copays. Higher tiers (brand-name, specialty) cost more. Different carriers cover different drugs at different tiers, so if you take medications, checking the formulary before enrolling is essential. I verify your medications are covered before you enroll.

### Can I use my health insurance in another state?
With private PPO plans, yes — they typically offer nationwide coverage across all 50 states. ACA marketplace plans are often limited to your state or local area. If you travel frequently or split time between states, a PPO plan is generally the better choice.

### What happens if I have health insurance through two plans (coordination of benefits)?
When you're covered by two plans, one is designated as primary and the other as secondary. The primary plan pays first, and the secondary may cover remaining costs. This is common when both spouses have employer coverage.

### What should I do if my health insurance claim is denied?
Contact me. I help clients navigate claim denials. You have the right to appeal, and insurance companies are required to provide a clear reason for denial. Many denials are overturned on appeal with proper documentation.

### Is short-term health insurance a good option?
Short-term plans are cheaper but come with significant limitations — they typically don't cover pre-existing conditions, may not include all essential health benefits, and have limited coverage periods. They can work as a temporary bridge but shouldn't replace comprehensive coverage. I help you understand the trade-offs.

## Private PPO Plans

### What is a PPO plan and why do most Florida agents not tell you about them?
A PPO (Preferred Provider Organization) is a health insurance plan that lets you see any in-network doctor or specialist without a referral, provides out-of-network coverage, and typically offers nationwide provider networks. Most Florida agents don't tell you about PPO plans because they're captive agents contracted with a single carrier that only sells marketplace HMO plans — they literally cannot offer you a PPO option. Only independent agents have access to both marketplace and private PPO plans.

### Are private PPO plans cheaper than ACA marketplace plans?
For many healthy individuals and families who don't qualify for significant ACA subsidies, private PPO plans can be substantially cheaper. Monthly premiums may be 30-50% lower than comparable marketplace plans. The trade-off is that coverage is underwritten based on health history. I compare both options to determine which saves you the most.

### Can I keep my doctor with a private PPO plan?
In most cases, yes. Private PPO plans from top national and regional carriers offer some of the largest provider networks in the country. Unlike narrow-network ACA plans, PPOs let you see any in-network doctor without a referral, and many provide out-of-network coverage as well. I verify your preferred providers are in-network before you enroll.

### Who qualifies for a private PPO health plan in Florida?
Private PPO plans are available to individuals and families who are not on Medicare or Medicaid. Unlike ACA plans, there is no open enrollment period — you can apply any time of year. Approval is based on health history and age. They're especially popular with self-employed individuals, small business owners, and healthy families looking for better coverage at lower rates.

### What is the difference between an HMO and a PPO plan?
HMOs have small, regional networks and require a primary care physician referral to see specialists. If you go out-of-network, you pay 100% (except emergencies). PPOs have broad, nationwide networks with no referral requirements and partial out-of-network coverage. PPOs cost the same to about 20-30% more in premiums but often have lower deductibles and out-of-pocket maximums.

### How do I switch health insurance plans without losing coverage?
With proper timing, there's no coverage gap. Align your new plan's start date with your old plan's end date. ACA marketplace plans can be switched during open enrollment (November 1 - January 15) or after a qualifying life event. Private PPO plans accept applications year-round. I coordinate timing to prevent any gaps.

### Are PPO plans available on the ACA marketplace?
Very few. The marketplace is dominated by HMO and EPO plans. True PPO plans with broad nationwide networks are almost exclusively available through the private market — which is why most people don't know they exist.

### Are PPO plans good for travel or living in multiple states?
Yes, this is a major advantage. PPO plans have nationwide networks that are ideal for travelers, snowbirds, remote workers, and people who split time between states. You can see any in-network provider across the country.

## ACA / Marketplace Plans

### How do ACA marketplace plans work in Florida?
ACA marketplace plans are health insurance plans offered through HealthCare.gov under the Affordable Care Act. In Florida, you can enroll during the annual open enrollment period (typically November through January) or during a special enrollment period triggered by a qualifying life event. Premium subsidies are available based on household income.

### Do I qualify for ACA premium subsidies in Florida?
You may qualify for premium tax credits if your household income falls between 100% and 400% of the federal poverty level. For a single individual in 2026, that's roughly between $15,000 and $62,000 per year. For a family of four, the range extends to approximately $127,000. I check your subsidy eligibility as part of every free consultation.

### When is open enrollment for ACA plans in Florida?
The annual open enrollment period typically runs from November 1 through January 15. Outside of open enrollment, you can only enroll if you experience a qualifying life event.

### Should I choose an ACA plan or a private PPO plan?
It depends on your income, health status, and coverage needs. If you qualify for significant premium subsidies, an ACA plan may be more affordable. If you're healthy, don't qualify for subsidies, and want broader networks with more flexibility, a private PPO plan often provides better value. I compare both options side by side during every free consultation.

### Does it cost extra to use an agent for ACA enrollment?
No. You pay the exact same premium whether you enroll on your own through HealthCare.gov or through an agent. The advantage is expert guidance on plan selection, subsidy optimization, and someone to call year-round.

### What is a qualifying life event for health insurance?
A qualifying life event (QLE) allows you to enroll in or change ACA marketplace coverage outside of open enrollment. Common QLEs include: getting married, having a baby or adopting, losing employer-sponsored coverage, moving to a new state or county, turning 26 and aging off a parent's plan, and losing Medicaid or CHIP eligibility. You typically have 60 days from the event to enroll. Private PPO plans don't require a QLE — you can apply year-round.

### Can I buy health insurance outside of open enrollment?
For ACA marketplace plans, you need a qualifying life event. Private PPO plans are available year-round with no enrollment windows.

### What is the difference between Bronze, Silver, Gold, and Platinum ACA plans?
These "metal tiers" represent different levels of cost-sharing:
- **Bronze:** Lowest premiums, highest deductibles ($7,000-$9,200). Good for healthy people who rarely use care.
- **Silver:** Moderate premiums and deductibles ($3,000-$6,000). Most popular. Eligible for cost-sharing reductions.
- **Gold:** Higher premiums, lower deductibles ($1,000-$2,500). Good for regular healthcare users.
- **Platinum:** Highest premiums, lowest out-of-pocket. Best for frequent healthcare users.

### What is a high-deductible health plan (HDHP) and how does it compare to a traditional plan?
An HDHP has a higher deductible than a traditional plan but lower monthly premiums. HDHPs are eligible for Health Savings Accounts (HSAs), which allow tax-free savings for medical expenses. They work well for healthy individuals who want lower premiums and are comfortable paying more out-of-pocket before insurance kicks in.

### What happens if I don't qualify for ACA subsidies in Florida?
If your household income exceeds the subsidy threshold, you pay the full unsubsidized premium — which averaged $652 per month for a Silver plan in Florida in 2026. Private PPO plans are often significantly cheaper in this situation, starting around $276 per month for a healthy 40-year-old, while also offering broader networks and nationwide coverage.

## Self-Employed & Small Business

### Do I need health insurance if I'm self-employed or a 1099 contractor?
Absolutely. Self-employed individuals have no employer safety net. A single ER visit or unexpected diagnosis without insurance can cost tens of thousands. The good news: self-employed people often have MORE options than W-2 employees because they can shop the entire market. Plus, you can deduct 100% of your health insurance premiums from your federal taxes.

### Can self-employed people in Florida get affordable health insurance?
Yes. Self-employed individuals frequently find that private PPO plans are more affordable than ACA marketplace plans, especially if they're in good health and earn too much for meaningful subsidies. Private PPO premiums for a healthy self-employed 40-year-old can start around $276 per month. You can also deduct 100% of premiums as a business expense on your federal tax return.

### What health insurance options do 1099 contractors have?
1099 contractors have several options: ACA marketplace plans (with potential subsidies), private PPO plans (year-round enrollment, broader networks), supplemental/gap coverage (accident, critical illness, hospital indemnity), income protection (replaces income if you can't work), and standalone dental and vision. Every dollar of premium is tax-deductible.

### Can I deduct health insurance premiums if I'm self-employed?
Yes. Self-employed individuals can deduct 100% of health insurance premiums (medical, dental, vision, long-term care for self, spouse, and dependents) as an above-the-line deduction on their federal tax return. This reduces your adjusted gross income directly — you get it whether you itemize or not. Example: $8,400/year in premiums in a 22% tax bracket saves you about $1,848 in federal taxes.

### Do Florida small businesses have to offer health insurance?
No. Only Applicable Large Employers (ALEs) with 50 or more full-time equivalent employees are legally required to offer health insurance. Small businesses under 50 employees have no mandate. But health insurance is consistently ranked as the most valued employee benefit, and it helps attract and retain talent.

### What is an ICHRA?
An Individual Coverage Health Reimbursement Arrangement allows businesses of any size to reimburse employees tax-free for individual health insurance premiums and medical expenses. The business sets a monthly allowance by employee class, employees choose their own plans, and submit receipts for reimbursement. It gives employers cost control and employees choice.

### Is group insurance or individual coverage cheaper for small businesses?
For many Florida small businesses with fewer than 10 employees, individual plans with an ICHRA or QSEHRA are more cost-effective than group insurance. Each employee finds their own plan tailored to their needs. Group insurance may be better for young, healthy workforces where the collective risk pool is favorable.

### Can a sole proprietor get group health insurance?
No. You need at least one W-2 employee (not yourself or your spouse) to qualify for a group health plan. Sole proprietors should use individual marketplace or private PPO plans, and can set up an ICHRA or QSEHRA once they hire employees.

### What is the Small Business Health Care Tax Credit?
Available to businesses with fewer than 25 full-time equivalent employees with average wages around $56,000, who pay at least 50% of employee premiums through SHOP marketplace plans. The credit can cover up to 50% of the employer's contribution (35% for tax-exempt organizations). Available for two consecutive years.

## Dental, Vision & Supplemental Coverage

### Do I need dental insurance if my health plan doesn't include it?
Yes, most health plans don't include comprehensive dental coverage. A separate dental plan covers cleanings, exams, fillings, crowns, and more. Without coverage, a single dental procedure can cost hundreds to thousands out of pocket. Individual dental plans typically range from $20 to $50 per month.

### How much does dental insurance cost in Florida?
Individual dental plans typically range from $20 to $50 per month. Plans generally cover 100% of preventive care, 80% of basic procedures, and 50% of major procedures.

### Can I get dental and vision coverage in one plan?
Yes. Many carriers offer bundled plans combining dental and vision coverage into a single policy at a discounted rate. Bundled plans typically run $25-$60 per month.

### Do dental and vision plans have waiting periods?
Many dental plans have 6-12 month waiting periods for major procedures. Preventive care is usually covered immediately. Vision plans typically have no waiting period.

### What does supplemental insurance cover?
Supplemental insurance pays cash benefits directly to you when you experience a covered event such as an accident, hospital stay, or critical illness diagnosis. You can use the money for anything — deductibles, copays, lost income, travel, or everyday bills.

### What is critical illness insurance?
Critical illness insurance pays a lump-sum cash benefit ($5,000-$100,000) if you're diagnosed with cancer, heart attack, stroke, organ failure, or other serious conditions. The money is paid directly to you and can be used however you need.

### Do I need supplemental insurance if I already have health insurance?
Health insurance covers medical bills, but not lost income, travel for treatment, childcare during recovery, or high deductibles. Supplemental insurance fills those gaps with cash benefits. It's especially valuable for people with high-deductible plans.

### How much does supplemental insurance cost?
Very affordable. Accident plans: $15-$30/month. Critical illness: $25-$75/month. Hospital indemnity: $20-$50/month. Cancer: $20-$60/month.

### What is income protection insurance and do I need it?
Income protection insurance replaces 50-70% of your salary if you're unable to work due to illness, injury, or a medical condition. Benefits begin after a short waiting period and can last 3-24 months. It's essential for self-employed individuals, 1099 contractors, and anyone without employer disability benefits.

### Do I need income protection if I'm self-employed?
Self-employed individuals have the greatest need because they have no employer disability benefits, sick leave, or PTO. If you can't work, your income stops immediately. Income protection keeps bills paid during recovery.

### How much does income protection insurance cost?
Typically 1-3% of your annual income. For someone earning $50,000/year, that's roughly $40-$125/month. Rates depend on age, occupation, health, benefit amount, and waiting period.

### What's the difference between short-term and long-term disability?
Short-term covers 3-24 months after a 7-14 day waiting period. Long-term kicks in after short-term ends and can provide benefits for years or until retirement. Most people start with short-term coverage because it's more affordable.

### What conditions does income protection cover?
Surgery recovery, serious illness, injuries from accidents, pregnancy complications, back problems, mental health conditions, and other medical situations requiring extended time away from work.

## Life Changes & Special Situations

### What is COBRA insurance and how long does it last?
COBRA allows you to continue your existing employer health plan for up to 18 months after leaving a job. You pay the entire premium plus a 2% administrative fee — typically $600-$1,800 per month. It can make sense if you're mid-treatment or have met your deductible, but it's usually 40-60% more expensive than private PPO or marketplace alternatives.

### How does divorce affect my health insurance coverage?
Divorce is a qualifying life event, so you can enroll in new coverage outside of open enrollment. If you were covered through your spouse's employer plan, you'll lose that coverage and need to find your own. I help people going through divorce compare all options and find affordable coverage quickly.

### Can I switch health insurance plans at any time?
For ACA marketplace plans, you can switch during open enrollment (November 1 - January 15) or after a qualifying life event. Private PPO plans are available year-round — you can apply any time.

## Blog-Sourced FAQs

### How can I lower my health insurance costs in Brevard County?
Compare across multiple carriers, check both marketplace and private options, review subsidy eligibility carefully, consider bundling dental/vision separately, don't over-insure or under-insure, and work with an independent agent who can see the full market.

### Does age affect health insurance costs?
Yes — it's the single biggest factor. Under ACA rules, there's a 3:1 age band, meaning a 64-year-old can be charged up to 3 times what a 21-year-old pays. A 25-year-old might pay $320/month for a Silver plan, while a 60-year-old pays around $850/month for the same plan before subsidies.

### Is COBRA worth it?
Usually expensive — you pay the full premium plus a 2% administrative fee, typically $600-$1,800/month. It can be useful as a short-term bridge if you're mid-treatment or have met your deductible, but private PPO or marketplace plans are usually significantly cheaper.

### Can I have both a private plan and an ACA marketplace plan?
Technically possible but rarely makes sense financially. Better to choose one and supplement with standalone dental/vision and supplemental coverage.

### Do private plans cover pre-existing conditions?
ACA-compliant private plans must cover pre-existing conditions. However, non-ACA plans (short-term, health sharing) may not. Always verify ACA compliance.

### What percentage of Brevard County is uninsured?
Approximately 12-14% of the non-elderly population, or roughly 55,000-65,000 residents. Many are self-employed, small business owners, part-time workers, gig workers, or between jobs — and many qualify for affordable coverage they don't know about.

### Can hospitals turn away uninsured patients?
No. All hospitals are required to provide emergency stabilization under EMTALA (Emergency Medical Treatment and Labor Act). But uninsured patients are billed at full retail rates — significantly higher than the negotiated rates insurance companies pay.

### What is a formulary?
A formulary is a carrier's list of covered medications organized into tiers. Tier 1 (generics) has the lowest copays. Higher tiers (brand-name, specialty) cost more. Different carriers cover different drugs at different costs, so checking the formulary before enrolling is essential.

### How does the self-employed health insurance deduction work?
It's an above-the-line deduction on your federal tax return — you get it whether you itemize or not. It directly reduces your adjusted gross income. Covers medical, dental, vision, and long-term care premiums for yourself, your spouse, and dependents. You cannot deduct for months you were eligible for employer coverage, and you cannot deduct more than your net self-employment income.

### What is a health sharing plan?
Health sharing plans are not insurance but member organizations that share medical costs. Monthly costs are typically $200-$500, less than traditional insurance. Caveats: not regulated like insurance, can decline pre-existing conditions, no legal guarantee of payment. Good for healthy individuals comfortable with the risk.

### Should I choose a higher deductible plan?
A higher-deductible plan means lower monthly premiums but higher out-of-pocket costs when you need care. HDHPs work well for healthy individuals who want to save on premiums and are eligible for Health Savings Accounts (HSAs). If you use healthcare regularly, a lower-deductible plan may save you more overall.

### Which health insurance carriers operate in Florida?
Florida's market includes multiple major national and regional carriers offering different plan types, network sizes, and pricing. Some specialize in large in-state networks, others focus on nationwide PPO coverage or competitive marketplace pricing. I'm contracted with all major Florida carriers and shop them simultaneously at no cost to you.
