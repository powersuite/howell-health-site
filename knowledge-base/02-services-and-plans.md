## 1. Private PPO Health Plans

### What Are They?
Private PPO health plans offer nationwide coverage through top-rated national and regional carriers. They provide broader networks, more flexibility, and often lower premiums than marketplace plans. These are the plans most people don't know exist — they're not listed on HealthCare.gov and you won't find them on the public exchanges. You only hear about them through an independent agent who has access to both markets.

### What Makes Private PPO Plans Different?
- **Broader provider networks** — PPO plans include thousands of doctors, hospitals, and specialists nationwide, not just within your zip code
- **No referral requirement** — See any specialist without needing a referral from a primary care physician
- **Out-of-network coverage** — Many PPO plans cover out-of-network providers at a reduced rate, giving you more flexibility
- **Year-round enrollment** — Unlike ACA plans, you can apply for private PPO coverage any time — no waiting for open enrollment
- **Often lower premiums** — For healthy individuals and families, private PPO premiums can be 30-50% lower than comparable ACA plans

### Who Are Private PPO Plans Best For?
- Self-employed individuals and 1099 contractors
- Small business owners without group coverage
- Families who want to keep their preferred doctors
- Healthy individuals who don't qualify for ACA subsidies
- Anyone between jobs who needs coverage immediately
- Early retirees not yet eligible for Medicare
- Frequent travelers and snowbirds who need nationwide coverage
- People who dislike referral requirements

### Private PPO vs. ACA Marketplace — Side-by-Side

| Feature | Private PPO Plans | ACA Marketplace Plans |
|---|---|---|
| Provider Network | Nationwide, broad PPO networks | Often narrow, limited to zip code |
| Enrollment Period | Apply any time, year-round | Open enrollment or qualifying event |
| Premium Subsidies | Not available | Available based on income |
| Premiums for Healthy Individuals | Often 30-50% lower | Standard rates regardless of health |
| Specialist Referrals | Not required | Often required (HMO plans) |
| Out-of-Network Coverage | Usually included | Rarely included |
| Pre-Existing Conditions | Underwritten (health-based approval) | Guaranteed issue (no denial) |
| Cost to Use an Agent | $0 — same price with or without | $0 — same price with or without |

### How PPO Plans Work
PPO (Preferred Provider Organization) plans are built around flexibility:
- **In-network providers:** Lowest rates with copays and coinsurance
- **Out-of-network providers:** Still covered, but at a higher share (typically 50-70% after deductible)
- **No referral requirement:** See specialists directly
- **No primary care gatekeeper:** Optional to designate a PCP

### PPO vs. HMO — Key Differences
- **Network size:** HMOs have small, regional networks (few-county radius). PPOs have national networks with hundreds of thousands of providers across all 50 states.
- **Referrals:** HMOs require a PCP gatekeeper and referral to see a specialist. PPOs allow direct specialist access.
- **Out-of-network:** HMOs pay 100% out of pocket if out-of-network (except emergencies). PPOs provide partial coverage for out-of-network care.

### Why Most Florida Agents Don't Tell You About PPOs
Most Florida agents are "captive" — they work for or are contracted exclusively with a single carrier that primarily sells marketplace HMO/EPO plans. They literally cannot offer private PPO plans because they don't have contracts with the carriers that sell them. Independent agents like me have contracts with multiple carriers across both the marketplace and private markets, so I can show you both options side by side.

## 2. ACA / Marketplace Plans

### What Are They?
ACA marketplace plans provide health coverage through HealthCare.gov with potential premium subsidies based on your income. I help you navigate enrollment, determine your subsidy eligibility, and compare marketplace plans across carriers to find the best coverage for your budget — at zero cost to you.

### Key Benefits of ACA Marketplace Plans
- **Guaranteed issue** — Cannot be denied coverage for pre-existing conditions or health history
- **Premium subsidies** — Tax credits available based on household income to reduce monthly premiums
- **Essential health benefits** — All plans must cover 10 essential health benefit categories including hospitalization, prescriptions, and preventive care
- **Standardized tiers** — Bronze, Silver, Gold, and Platinum tiers make it easier to compare coverage levels
- **Preventive care at no cost** — Annual check-ups, screenings, and vaccinations covered at $0 cost-sharing

### Who Should Consider an ACA Marketplace Plan?
- Individuals and families who qualify for premium subsidies
- People with pre-existing conditions who may not qualify for private plans
- Those losing employer-sponsored coverage
- Young adults aging off a parent's plan at 26
- Anyone experiencing a qualifying life event outside open enrollment

### ACA Metal Tiers Explained
- **Bronze:** Lowest premiums, highest deductibles ($7,000-$9,200). Best for healthy individuals who rarely visit the doctor.
- **Silver:** Moderate premiums and deductibles ($3,000-$6,000). Most popular tier. Qualifies for cost-sharing reductions if income is under 250% of the federal poverty level.
- **Gold:** Higher premiums, lower deductibles ($1,000-$2,500). Better for people who use healthcare regularly.
- **Platinum:** Highest premiums, lowest out-of-pocket costs.

### Subsidy Eligibility
You may qualify for premium tax credits if your household income falls between 100% and 400% of the federal poverty level:
- **Single individual (2026):** Roughly $15,000 to $62,000 per year
- **Family of four (2026):** Up to approximately $127,000 per year
- Subsidies can reduce a $650/month Silver plan to $100 or less in some cases

### Open Enrollment
Annual open enrollment for ACA marketplace plans in Florida typically runs **November 1 through January 15**. Outside of open enrollment, you can only enroll with a qualifying life event (losing coverage, moving, marriage, baby, turning 26).

### 2026 Market Context
The Enhanced Premium Tax Credits from the Inflation Reduction Act are set to expire in 2026. Without congressional action, many families earning above 400% of the federal poverty level will lose subsidies entirely, and those below the threshold will see reduced assistance. This is expected to drive significant marketplace premium increases.

## 3. Dental & Vision

### Why You Need Separate Coverage
Most health insurance plans — both private PPO and ACA marketplace — do not include comprehensive dental or vision coverage. Without insurance:
- A dental crown can cost $1,000 or more
- Prescription glasses can run $300-$600
- These costs come entirely out of pocket

### What Dental Insurance Covers
- **Preventive care (100% covered):** Cleanings, X-rays, oral exams — typically twice per year
- **Basic procedures (~80% covered):** Fillings, extractions, and simple restorations
- **Major procedures (~50% covered):** Crowns, bridges, root canals, and dentures
- **Orthodontics (select plans):** Braces and aligners for children and adults

### What Vision Insurance Covers
- Annual eye exams with low or no copay
- Allowances for glasses frames, lenses, and contact lenses
- Discounts on LASIK and other corrective procedures
- Coverage for lens enhancements (anti-reflective, progressive)

### Standalone vs. Bundled Plans

| Option | Standalone Plans | Bundled Plans |
|---|---|---|
| Cost | $15-$50/mo per plan | $25-$60/mo for both |
| Flexibility | Choose only what you need | All-in-one convenience |
| Savings | Pay only for what you use | Bundled discount vs. separate |
| Best For | People needing only one type | Families and comprehensive coverage |

### Typical Costs
- **Individual dental plans:** $20-$50 per month
- **Bundled dental + vision:** $25-$60 per month

### Waiting Periods
- Many dental plans have 6-12 month waiting periods for major procedures
- Preventive care is usually covered immediately
- Vision plans typically have no waiting period

## 4. Supplemental & Critical Illness

### Why Supplemental Coverage Matters
Your health insurance covers medical bills — but what about everything else? When a serious illness or accident happens, the financial impact goes far beyond hospital costs:
- Lost wages
- Travel for treatment
- Childcare during recovery
- High deductibles
- Everyday bills that don't stop

Supplemental insurance fills those gaps with **cash benefits paid directly to you**. No restrictions on how you use the money.

### Types of Supplemental Insurance

**Critical Illness Insurance**
- Lump-sum payment ($5,000-$100,000) upon diagnosis of cancer, heart attack, stroke, organ failure, or other covered conditions
- Typical cost: $25-$75/month

**Accident Insurance**
- Cash benefits for injuries from accidents — broken bones, dislocations, ER visits, ambulance rides, follow-up care
- Typical cost: $15-$30/month

**Hospital Indemnity Insurance**
- Daily cash benefit ($100-$500/day) for each day you're hospitalized, regardless of the reason
- Typical cost: $20-$50/month

**Cancer Insurance**
- Targeted coverage specifically for cancer diagnosis, treatment, and recovery expenses
- Typical cost: $20-$60/month

### Who Needs Supplemental Coverage?
- Anyone with a high-deductible health plan (HDHP)
- Families living on a tight budget with limited emergency savings
- Self-employed individuals who can't afford to miss work
- People with family history of cancer, heart disease, or stroke
- Active individuals with higher accident risk
- Anyone who wants peace of mind beyond basic health coverage

### How I Help
I help you determine which types of supplemental insurance make sense for your situation and budget, then compare options across carriers. Free consultation.

## 5. Income Protection

### What Is It?
Income protection insurance replaces a portion of your paycheck — typically 50-70% of your salary — if illness or injury prevents you from working. Your ability to earn a living is your most valuable financial asset. If an illness or injury kept you from working for three months, could you pay your mortgage, utilities, groceries, and other bills? For most Americans, the answer is no.

### How Income Protection Works
- **Benefit amount:** Typically replaces 50-70% of your pre-disability income
- **Waiting period:** Benefits begin after a short elimination period, usually 7-14 days
- **Benefit period:** Short-term plans pay benefits for 3-24 months; long-term plans can extend for years
- **Covered conditions:** Surgery recovery, serious illness, injuries, pregnancy complications, back problems, mental health conditions, and more
- **Tax-free benefits:** When you pay premiums with after-tax dollars, benefits are generally received tax-free

### Short-Term vs. Long-Term Disability

| Feature | Short-Term Disability | Long-Term Disability |
|---|---|---|
| Waiting Period | 7-14 days | 90-180 days |
| Benefit Period | 3-24 months | 2 years to age 65+ |
| Income Replacement | 50-70% | 50-60% |
| Monthly Cost | $40-$125/mo | $50-$200/mo |
| Best For | Common illnesses, injuries, surgery | Long-term conditions, chronic illness |
| Application | Usually simplified underwriting | Full medical underwriting |

### Who Needs Income Protection?
- Self-employed individuals and 1099 contractors with no employer benefits
- Small business owners who are the primary revenue generator
- Families with a single income earner
- Anyone without adequate emergency savings (less than 6 months' expenses)
- Workers in physically demanding occupations
- Employees whose employer doesn't offer disability coverage

### Typical Cost
About 1-3% of your annual income. For someone earning $50,000/year, that's roughly $40-$125/month.

## Small Business Health Insurance Options

For Florida small business owners, I also help navigate these additional options:

### ICHRA (Individual Coverage Health Reimbursement Arrangement)
- Business of any size reimburses employees tax-free for individual premiums and medical expenses
- Business sets monthly allowance by employee class
- Employees choose their own individual plans
- Full tax advantages for both employer and employee

### QSEHRA (Qualified Small Employer HRA)
- Simpler version of ICHRA for businesses with fewer than 50 employees
- Annual reimbursement limits: ~$6,150 individual, ~$12,450 family (2026)

### Small Group Health Insurance
- For businesses with 2-50 employees
- Employer selects plan(s), pays a portion, employee pays the rest
- Most Florida carriers require minimum 50% employer contribution

### SHOP Marketplace Plans
- For 1-50 employees
- Primary advantage: Small Business Health Care Tax Credit (up to 50% of contributions)
- Eligibility: fewer than 25 FTE with average wages around $56,000

### Self-Employed Health Insurance Tax Deduction
Self-employed individuals can deduct 100% of health insurance premiums (medical, dental, vision, long-term care) from their federal taxes as an above-the-line deduction — meaning you get it whether you itemize or not.
