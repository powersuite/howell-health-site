## The 4 Qualifying Questions

During our conversation, I ask these key questions to understand your situation and find the best options:

### 1. What type of coverage are you looking for?
- **Individual** — Just yourself
- **Family** — You, spouse, and/or children
- **Business / Self-Employed** — Coverage for yourself as a business owner, or for you and employees

*Why I ask:* The type of coverage determines which plan structures, pricing tiers, and tax advantages apply. A solo individual has different options than a family of five or a business owner looking at ICHRAs.

### 2. Where are you located and who needs coverage?
- **Zip code** — This determines your geographic rating area, which affects premiums and which carriers/plans are available
- **Ages of everyone who needs coverage** — Age is the single biggest factor in health insurance pricing

*Why I ask:* A 30-year-old in Melbourne gets very different rates than a 55-year-old in Titusville. I need accurate ages and location to pull real quotes from carriers.

### 3. Any specifics about your coverage needs?
- **Coverage level preference:** Basic/catastrophic, mid-level (most popular), comprehensive/premium, or "not sure — help me decide"
- **Existing medical conditions:** Optional, but helps me find plans with the best coverage for specific needs

*Why I ask:* Someone who's healthy and rarely sees a doctor might do great with a higher-deductible plan. Someone managing a chronic condition needs comprehensive coverage. Pre-existing conditions also affect whether private PPO plans are an option or whether ACA marketplace is the better path.

### 4. Do you have preferred doctors or hospitals?
- **Specific doctor names and practice names**
- **Preferred hospital or health system** (e.g., Holmes Regional, Parrish Medical Center)

*Why I ask:* This is the #1 reason people fear switching plans. I cross-reference your provider list against every carrier's network before recommending anything. You'll never get surprised by an out-of-network bill.

## How the Consultation Works

### Before the Call — What to Have Ready
- Ages of everyone who needs coverage
- Your zip code
- Names of your current doctors and preferred hospital (if any)
- List of current medications (if any)
- Your current plan details (if you have coverage now) — helpful but not required
- A general sense of your household income (relevant for ACA subsidy eligibility)
- Any specific coverage concerns or priorities

### The Call Itself — What to Expect

**Duration:** About 20 minutes — quick and focused.

**Tone:** Casual, friendly, zero pressure. Think neighbor giving you advice, not a sales pitch.

**What happens:**
1. I ask about your situation — who needs coverage, what you're currently paying, what matters most to you
2. I ask the 4 qualifying questions conversationally (not like a form — it's a real conversation)
3. I explain the difference between private PPO and ACA marketplace options as they relate to your situation
4. I ask about preferred doctors and hospitals so I can verify networks
5. I explain what happens next

**What does NOT happen:**
- No hard sell or pressure tactics
- No commitment required
- No credit card or payment information collected
- No obligation of any kind

### After the Call — What Happens Next

1. **I shop the market** — I pull quotes from top-rated national and regional carriers for both private PPO and ACA marketplace options (if applicable)
2. **I verify your providers** — I check that your preferred doctors and hospitals are in-network with the plans I'm recommending
3. **I check your medications** — I verify formulary coverage for any prescriptions you take
4. **I present your options** — Usually within a few hours, I'll share a side-by-side comparison of your best options with clear pricing
5. **You decide** — No rush, no pressure. You pick the plan that fits, or you decide to keep what you have. Either way, I'm here to help.
6. **I handle the paperwork** — If you choose a plan, I manage the entire enrollment process
